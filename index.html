How it works

Built for medicine applicants and students

Understanding mechanisms — not just facts — is what separates good doctors from great ones. Each day brings a new mechanism designed to build that intuition.

📖

Learn

Every mechanism is broken into 3 clear steps. Reveal them one at a time, then unlock the full clinical explanation and a memory hook that makes it stick.

🎯

Guess

Three daily challenges — Easy, Medium, Hard — each with a different mechanism. You get 3 attempts before the full solution is revealed. Drawn arrow-pushing diagrams included.

🔥

Streak

Come back every day to build your streak. Your progress is saved automatically — no account needed, no data collected, nothing to sign up for.

Three difficulty modes

A different mechanism for each mode, every day

Easy, Medium, and Hard each pull from separate pools — so you're always learning something new, never repeating the same mechanism twice in one day.

🟢

Easy

Read the first step of a mechanism and identify it from 4 options. Wrong answers are eliminated. Perfect for building recognition.

🟡

Medium

Given a category and tags, type the mechanism name. Hints unlock after wrong guesses. Drawn diagrams appear to help you reason through it.

🔴

Hard

Tags only, no clue, minimal hints. College-level chemistry mechanisms included — benzene reactions, SN2, carbonyl chemistry, amide formation.

The story behind it

Why I built this

I'm a sixth-form student applying to study medicine. Like a lot of applicants, I spent months reading, revising, and trying to prepare — but I kept noticing the same gap. I could memorise that aspirin is an antiplatelet drug. But understanding why — that it permanently acetylates COX-1, that platelets can't replace it because they have no nucleus, that this is why the effect lasts 7–10 days and why you stop it before surgery — that's what actually makes the knowledge useful.

"Understanding the mechanism is the difference between knowing a fact and being able to think with it."

I looked for something like the Daily Integral — that beautiful daily puzzle format — but for medicine. Nothing quite existed. So I built it. The Daily Mechanism covers pharmacology, biochemistry, and organic chemistry, always with a clinical connection so you can see why it actually matters.

Every mechanism has been written to answer three questions: what happens (the steps), so what (the clinical relevance), and how do I remember this (the memory hook). It's the format I wish I'd had.

If you're an aspiring medic, a first-year student, or just someone who finds this stuff genuinely interesting — this site is for you. It's completely free and always will be. Come back tomorrow for a new one.
